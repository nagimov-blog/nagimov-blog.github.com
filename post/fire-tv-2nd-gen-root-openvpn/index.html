Amazon Fire TV 2nd Gen - Root & OpenVPN

2016-03-30 in using tags Amazon , Fire TV , Kodi , XBMC , VPN , OpenVPN , Android , linker , PIE , Root

My notes after spending half a day playing with my Fire TV.

While Amazon Fire TV seems to be the best htpc solution to me, it still requires rooting and a bit of patching on its way to a decent media center. I’m also used to US streaming services (Pandora, Netflix, Hulu, etc) which are only available through VPN, if you are outside of US. Rooting and setting up Kodi + OpenVPN is a way to go.

Rooting is simple and straightforward, I followed this guide and everything went smooth.

Problems started with OpenVPN. Even though there are few tutorials out there (here and there), I didn’t manage to install OpenVPN using the installer apk.

Another tutorial suggested to do manually everything what installer is supposed to do (i.e. unpack OpenVPN binary to /system/xbin/). So I’ve done that and got only position independent executables (PIE) are supported error when trying to start OpenVPN from adb console. Apparently, there is a big issue with position independent executables (whatever that means). The first fix is to find the OpenVPN binary compiled with PIE support (or compile it by yourself adding "-fPIE -pie" linker option), but people are lazy, apparently. Another way to deal with that PIE issue is to patch the linker binary located in /system/bin/ directory. I’ve tried a bunch of different versions of the linker file, sometimes getting an unexpected behavior of Android (e.g. application storage space is full error) or even screwing my Fire TV to the boot-loop. This is the version of patched linker which worked great with my Fire TV.

Just as a reminder, here is how you replace linker file in /system/bin/ directory:

adb push linker /sdcard/
adb shell
su
mount -o remount rw /system
mv /system/bin/linker /system/bin/linker.bak
cp /sdcard/linker /system/bin/
chmod 755 /system/bin/linker 
mount -o remount ro /system
rm /sdcard/linker

Happy Kodiing!
